Cajun Pickled Pork – Savoie’s / Richard’s Style

This recipe closely replicates the flavor, color, and texture of classic Louisiana commercial brands like Savoie’s and Richard’s pickled pork. It uses Prague Powder #1 for proper curing, a fattier pork shoulder for authentic texture, and paprika/chili color enhancement for the characteristic reddish brine.

Preferred Cut

Use boneless pork shoulder (pork butt). This cut has enough fat and myoglobin to produce the correct rosy color and tender texture after curing. Avoid lean cuts like loin, which produce a paler brine and firmer texture.

Ingredients (for 1 kg pork shoulder)

  • 1 kg boneless pork shoulder (whole or cut into large 4 cm chunks)
  • 500 mL distilled white vinegar
  • 500 mL water
  • 19 g kosher salt (about 3 tsp)
  • 2.5 g Prague Powder #1 (about ½ tsp level)
  • 5 g sugar (1 tsp)
  • 1 tbsp mustard seed
  • ½ tsp celery seed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 6 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 10–12 black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp Louisiana hot sauce (Crystal or Louisiana brand)
  • ¾ tsp Zatarain’s Liquid Crab Boil
  • ⅓ tsp sweet paprika
  • ⅛ tsp mild red chili powder (not cayenne)

Method

  • Combine vinegar, water, kosher salt, sugar, mustard seed, celery seed, bay leaves, garlic, peppercorns, hot sauce, Zatarain’s, paprika, and chili powder in a non-reactive pot.
  • Bring to a boil and simmer for 3–4 minutes to release flavors. Remove from heat and let the brine cool completely to room temperature.
  • Stir in Prague Powder #1 only after the brine has cooled. Never add curing salt to hot liquid.
  • Place the pork in a glass, stainless, or food-grade plastic container. Pour the cooled brine over until fully submerged.
  • Transfer meat and brine into a vacuum-sealable bag. Seal tightly, ensuring minimal air remains.
  • Cure under refrigeration (34–40 °F / 1–4 °C) for 7 days, flipping the bag every day or two for even curing.
  • After 7–10 days, remove the pork from the brine. Rinse lightly to remove excess seeds and spices.
  • Slice, cube, or leave whole as desired. The pork is cured but raw—cook thoroughly before eating.

Vacuum Sealing Instructions

Vacuum sealing prevents oxygen from oxidizing the cured pigments, helping the brine stay deep red. After pouring the cooled brine and pork into the bag, use a vacuum sealer on ‘moist’ or ‘gentle’ mode to remove air and seal. Store flat in the refrigerator, turning every day or two to ensure even exposure.

Storage After Curing

After curing, there are two storage options:

  • **Refrigerator (Short-Term)** – Keep sealed in the same brine and vacuum bag. Store at 34–38 °F (1–3 °C). Use within 10–14 days total (counting from start of cure).
  • **Freezer (Long-Term)** – For long storage, freeze directly in the brine inside the sealed bag, or remove from brine, pat dry, and vacuum seal again. Freeze at 0 °F (–18 °C). Use within 3–4 months for best quality.

When thawing, always thaw in the refrigerator. Once thawed, use within 3–5 days and cook thoroughly.

Notes

  • The reddish color will deepen over several days as the cure reacts fully with the meat.
  • Paprika and mild chili powder give the same warm tone used in commercial Louisiana pickle meat.
  • Always maintain refrigeration throughout curing.
  • Do not exceed the recommended Prague Powder #1 amount.

Kalabasa Pumpkin Pie Recipe

This Filipino-inspired pumpkin pie uses kalabasa (squash) instead of pumpkin. It’s creamy, perfectly spiced, and has a naturally sweet flavor that rivals the traditional version.

🧁 Ingredients

  • 2 cups kalabasa purée (steamed or roasted and mashed)
  • 1 can (300–400 ml) sweetened condensed milk OR 1 can evaporated milk + ½ cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp butter or coconut cream
  • ¾ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • tsp ground cloves
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • Optional: ½ tsp vanilla extract

🥣 How to Prepare Kalabasa for Pie

  • Peel and deseed about 1 kg of kalabasa.
  • Steam or roast until tender (steaming keeps it moist; roasting adds a caramel flavor).
  • Mash or blend until smooth. You should get around 2 cups of purée.
  • If it’s watery, strain it through cheesecloth or simmer briefly to thicken.

Instructions

  • Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  • In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, and sugar.
  • Add kalabasa purée, butter, spices, and vanilla. Mix until smooth.
  • Pour mixture into an unbaked pie shell (see recipe entitled “Basic Pie Shell Recipe”).
  • Bake for 45–55 minutes or until the center is set and a knife inserted comes out clean.
  • Cool completely before slicing. Serve with whipped cream or condensed milk drizzle.

🌿 Tips

  • Some people blend in a tablespoon of butter or coconut cream to enrich the flavor.
  • Kalabasa pie actually tends to hold its shape better than pumpkin pie.
  • Great with whipped cream, condensed milk drizzle, or a sprinkle of toasted coconut.

Tarte à la Bouille (Cajun Custard) Pie

By Ed Becnel

Ingredients

Custard Filling:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ¾ cup whole milk
  • ½ cup evaporated milk or another ½ cup of whole milk
  • 6 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 large egg, whisked
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter or margarine
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Crust:

  • 1 - 9 ½” pie pan
  • 1¾ cups of all-purpose flour + extra for working the dough to prevent sticking
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1½ teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 medium to large egg, whisked
  • ½ cup cold unsalted butter – cubed
  • ½ to 1 tablespoon whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Custard Filling:

  • In a cold saucepan, whisk together the whole milk, cream, evaporated milk, cornstarch, egg, sugar, and vanilla extract.
  • Place pan over medium high heat to start.
  • Gradually lower the heat to medium low as the mixture thickens to prevent burning.
  • Continue to whisk until the mixture thickens to the consistency of a thick custard.
  • Remove from the heat and whisk in the butter.
  • Cover the surface of the custard with plastic wrap and allow it to cool.

Crust:

  • In a large bowl whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and pinch of salt.
  • Cut in the cold butter and continue to mix until the mixture becomes crumbly.
  • Add the whisked egg and continue to mix.
  • Blend the vanilla extract with ½ tablespoon of milk. Mix well into the flour and egg mixture.
  • Add more milk if too dry or more flour if too wet. Mix until the dough comes together. Dough should be slightly moist but not sticky and can be molded into a ball.
  • Turn it out onto a clean lightly floured surface and using your hands, work the dough until it forms a ball. Dust with flour to prevent sticking.
  • Shape into a disk, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 4 to 6 hours or overnight.

Final steps for baking:

  • Preheat oven to 350°F. *Use only the lower burner and place the pie on the center oven rack.
  • Remove the pie crust from the refrigerator and plastic wrap.
  • Lightly butter the bottom of a 9 ½ inch pie pan (to prevent sticking).
  • Roll out about of the dough on a lightly floured strip of waxed paper to about 13 to 14 inches in diameter. Keep dusting the dough and roller to prevent sticking. The dough should be slightly more than “ thick.
  • Place the pie pan upside down centered over the rolled-out dough. Slide the waxed paper with the rolled-out dough and pie pan over an upside-down baking sheet or other flat surface. Then carefully turn the baking sheet with the waxed paper, dough, and pie pan right side up.
  • Carefully remove the waxed paper. Gently lift the edges of the pie dough up enough to allow the dough to sink into the pie pan. Work around the edges until the dough is settled into the pan.
  • There should be about 1 to 2 inches of shell extended beyond the edge of the pan. Trim off any excess evenly so that the same amount (about 1 inch) extends beyond the edge of the pan.
  • Roll out the remaining dough to a bit over inch thickness and cut into ¾ inch wide strips.
  • Pour the custard filling into the pie pan until it just fills the shell. Be careful not to overfill.
  • Lay the dough strips in a crosshatch pattern over the filling.
  • Fold the excess pastry dough hanging over the edges of the pan over the top of the pie to form a hexagon shape.
  • Bake for 25 - 35 minutes until the crust is golden and slightly brown on the edges and the custard filling is set and not jiggly.
  • Cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving.

Crispy Lemon-Chili Beef Shank

Shredded, pan-crisped beef shank with a spicy-tangy braise made from lemon and dried Asian/Korean/Chinese chilies. This is NOT stew. You braise, shred, reduce the liquid, then fry chunks until the edges go crispy and sticky.

Ingredients

For the beef:

~1.5–2 lb (0.7–1 kg) beef shank (bone-in or thick cross-cut pieces)

Salt and black pepper

~1 tbsp neutral oil for browning

For the braise sauce (to blend):

Chilies Option 1:

3–5 dried Asian/Chinese/Korean red chilies, seeds mostly removed to control heat; if they are extremely hot (tiny bird-type chilies), start with 2–3 instead of 5

Chilies Option 2:

1 dried ancho chili (mild, earthy)

1 dried guajillo chili (or more ancho if that’s what you have)

½ cup (120 ml) beef stock or water

1 tbsp tomato paste

1 tbsp apple cider vinegar, cane vinegar, or rice vinegar

Juice of ½ lime OR about ¼ – lemon (~ –1 tbsp / ~10–15 ml); save the rest in case you want a touch more brightness later

½ small onion, roughly chopped

3 cloves garlic

1 tsp dried oregano

1 tsp ground cumin

½ tsp smoked paprika (or regular paprika / mild chili powder)

¼ tsp ground cinnamon

½ tsp soy sauce (optional but helps replace the umami depth of ancho/guajillo)

1 bay leaf (DO NOT blend this; it goes in whole later)

1 tsp salt (to start)

About ½ tsp black pepper

Directions

  • Prep the dried chilies: Break open the chilies and shake/scrape out most of the seeds to dial down the heat. Lightly toast the chilies in a dry pan over medium heat ~20–30 seconds per side until fragrant (do not burn). Soak them in hot water 10–15 minutes to soften, then drain. (If you're using already-soft Korean chilies, just do a brief soak or skip soaking.)
  • Blend the braise sauce: In a blender, combine the softened chilies, beef stock/water, tomato paste, vinegar, lemon juice, onion, garlic, oregano, cumin, smoked paprika, cinnamon, soy sauce (if using), salt, and black pepper. Blend completely smooth. Taste: it should be bold, salty, tangy, and a little spicy. If it's flat, add a tiny splash more lemon. If it's too spicy, add a bit more stock and a pinch more tomato paste and re-blend.
  • Brown the beef: Pat the beef shank dry and season with salt and pepper. Heat the neutral oil in a heavy pot or Dutch oven over medium-high. Sear the shank hard on all sides until richly browned. Browning is crucial for depth.
  • Start the braise: Pour the blended sauce into the pot with the browned beef. Drop in the bay leaves (whole). The liquid should come at least halfway up the meat; add a little more water/stock if needed. Cover with a lid. Cook low and slow at 300°F / 150°C (oven) OR a gentle stovetop simmer for ~3 to 3.5 hours, flipping the shank once or twice. It's done when a fork twists in the meat and it basically falls apart and the connective tissue is melty instead of rubbery.
  • Shred and reduce: Pull the meat out to cool slightly. Remove bones and any big tough connective pieces. Shred or chunk the meat into bite-size pieces (not super fine). Meanwhile, put the pot with the braising liquid back on medium heat UNCOVERED and simmer to reduce until it thickens into a sauce that coats a spoon. Taste and adjust salt. Add just a FEW DROPS more lemon only if you want a brighter edge — it should not taste 'lemony', just alive.
  • Crisp the meat: Heat a wide skillet on medium-high. Add a single layer of shredded beef. Spoon a little of the reduced sauce over it. Let it fry without stirring so the bottom caramelizes and gets crispy-chewy. Flip once and crisp the other side. The collagen and sauce turn into crunchy, sticky, almost 'carnitas' edges.

Serving ideas: Stuff into tortillas with diced onion and cilantro/scallions; serve over plain rice with sliced cucumber and a squeeze of lemon; pile on garlic fried rice with a fried egg; or load into a baked potato with cheese.

Notes & Tips

• Heat control: Most Asian dried chilies are hotter than ancho or guajillo. Removing seeds dials spice down without changing flavor.

• Salt balance: The blended sauce should taste a little salty/strong on its own because it's seasoning a big piece of meat plus added liquid.

• Leftovers: Day-two leftovers re-crisp beautifully in a pan and may taste even better than day one.

Sticky Soy-Ginger Star Anise Beef Shank

Deep, glossy, savory-sweet braised beef shank. Sized for about 1.5–2 lb of beef shank. You brown, braise low and slow, then reduce the liquid into a shiny lacquered sauce.

Ingredients

~1.5–2 lb (0.7–1 kg) beef shank, bone-in or thick-cut cross sections

~1 tbsp neutral oil

cup (about 40 ml) soy sauce (≈2½ tbsp)

1 tbsp dark soy sauce (optional; if you don't have it, use 1 tbsp more regular soy + ½ tsp molasses or brown sugar)

1½ tbsp brown sugar or palm sugar (packed)

½ tbsp honey (about 1½ tsp)

2 tbsp (30 ml) rice vinegar or apple cider vinegar

About ½ a thumb-sized piece fresh ginger, sliced

3 cloves garlic, smashed

1 whole star anise

½ of a small cinnamon stick (or ~ tsp ground cinnamon)

½ tsp coarsely cracked black pepper

1 cup (240 ml) water or beef stock

1 scallion, cut into 2-inch chunks (optional but nice)

  • To finish / garnish:

Thinly sliced fresh scallions or chives

Toasted sesame seeds (optional)

Hot steamed rice or greens on the side

Directions

  • Brown the beef: Pat the beef shank dry and season lightly with salt and pepper. Heat a heavy pot or Dutch oven over medium-high with the neutral oil. Sear the shank well on all sides until you get a deep brown crust. Take your time here — browning builds flavor.
  • Build the braise liquid: Add the sliced ginger, smashed garlic, star anise, cinnamon, and cracked black pepper into the same pot with the browned bits. Cook ~30 seconds to wake up the aromatics. Pour in the soy sauce, dark soy (if using), brown sugar, honey, vinegar, and the water/stock. Scrape the bottom of the pot so all the browned fond dissolves. Add the scallion chunks. The liquid should come about halfway up the meat (add a splash more water if needed).
  • Slow braise: Cover with a lid. Either keep it at a gentle stovetop simmer on low OR move the covered pot to a 300°F / 150°C oven. Cook 2.5–3 hours, flipping the shank halfway, until a fork twists in the meat with almost no resistance. Even though this is a smaller batch, do not rush the time — you still need low and slow so the collagen breaks down.
  • Reduce to a glaze: Remove the shank to a board and let it rest. Turn the heat under the pot up to medium-high and boil the braising liquid UNCOVERED until it thickens into a shiny sauce that coats a spoon. Taste and adjust: if it's too salty, add a splash of water and simmer 1 more minute.
  • Slice and finish: Slice or chunk the beef across the grain (it will be very tender). Spoon the glossy reduced sauce over the top. Garnish with scallions and sesame seeds.

Serving ideas: Serve over jasmine rice or noodles with something bitter/green (bok choy, broccoli rabe, sautéed cabbage). Leftovers can be shredded, pan-fried until crisp, glazed with a spoon of that sauce, and stuffed into steamed buns or tortillas the next day.

Ed’s Cajun Andouille Sausage

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs.) pork butt - 70% lean 30% fat
  • 13 g (2 heaping teaspoons) kosher, pink, or sea salt
  • 2½ g (½ level teaspoon) pink cure #1
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 4.5 g (2 teaspoons) cracked black pepper.
  • 2 g to 4 g (1 teaspoon to 2 teaspoons) cayenne pepper (4g/2 teaspoons for spicy)
  • 7 g (2 cloves) minced fresh garlic.
  • 1 ½ teaspoons white sugar
  • ½ teaspoon to 1 teaspoon chili flakes (1 teaspoon for spicy)
  • ½ teaspoon granulated garlic
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ¼ teaspoon dried basil
  • ¼ teaspoon mustard powder
  • ¼ teaspoon white pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon sage and/or mace
  • 50 g of milk powder (5% weight of meat)
  • Cold water

Instructions

  • Cut lean pieces of pork into 1” to 2” pieces, freeze partially, and grind them through a ½” (12 mm) to ¾” (18 mm) plate.
  • Grind the fattier pieces through a ¼” (6 mm) plate.
  • In a large bowl, mix the meat with all the dry ingredients.
  • Mix well for about 5 minutes then gradually add water until the mixture is slightly sticky.
  • Mix for another 5 minutes.
  • Stuff into hog casings and tie to make 12” links.
  • Cure in the refrigerator for 12 to 24 hours.
  • Dry for 2 hours at room temperature then place in a pre-heated smoker at 130°F without smoke for 1 hour.
  • Smoke with pecan or apple wood mixed with hickory at around 140°F for about 5 hours until you obtain a nice rich mahogany color.
  • Cook Andouille in a smoker at 170°F to 195°F until the internal temperature reaches 154°F. This should take about 40 to 60 minutes.

Ed’s Cajun Chicken and Andouille Gumbo

Ingredients

Roux

½ cup all purpose flour

½ cup olive oil

Vegetables

1 medium onion finely - diced

1 medium to large green bell pepper finely - diced

2 stalks of celery finely - diced

4 medium garlic cloves finely - chopped

Meat

2 slices of bacon - chopped

2 teaspoons “Better than Bouillon” chicken base or 2 cubes of chicken bouillon

4 skinless bone-in chicken thighs seasoned with Creole or Cajun Seasoning (Tony Chachere’s preferred) (Figure 1 - Seasoned chicken thighs)

1 pound of andouille sausage or smoked sausage (Cajun or Polska Kielbasa) - whole

Liquid

32 ounces of chicken stock or broth

1 cup of water

Dry Seasonings

1 tablespoon of Creole or Cajun Seasoning

1 teaspoon smoked paprika

1 teaspoon granulated garlic

½ teaspoon coarse black pepper

¼ teaspoon dry thyme or 1 teaspoon fresh thyme

Liquid Seasonings

1 teaspoon liquid smoke (optional)

1 teaspoon kitchen bouquet (optional)

Cooking Instructions

Brown the chicken

Add the chopped bacon to a skillet with about 2 teaspoons of olive oil and saute until most of the fat is rendered off (Figure 2 - Brown bacon)

Add the seasoned chicken thighs and brown on both sides (about 5 to 7 minutes per side) (Figure 3 - Brown chicken)

When finished browning, deglaze the skillet with about 1 to 2 cups of water (Figure 4 - Deglaze chicken)

Reserve until the roux is done

Cook the roux

Add the flour and oil to either a cast iron pot, dutch oven, or wok over medium heat (Figure 5 - Start roux)

Continuously whisk the flour and oil while cooking to cook evenly and avoid burning

Adjust the heat to avoid burning and to facilitate browning the roux evenly

The roux should be the consistency of wet sand – not to dry but not too oily

If too oily, add flour

If too dry, add olive oil

If not browning, increase the heat

If smoking, decrease the heat

The roux is finished browning when it is the color of milk chocolate (Figure 6 - Roux is done)

After the roux is finished browning, stir in the chopped vegetables and the dry seasoning (Figure 8 - Add seasoning to roux)

Cook the vegetables in the roux, stirring frequently to cook evenly and avoid burning

The vegetables are finished cooking when they become translucent and slightly browned

Cook the gumbo

Add some of the stock to the roux and stir (Figure 10 - Add some of the stock to the roux)

Add the chicken with the deglazing liquid and whole links of sausage to the roux and stir well (Figure 11 - Add deglazed chicken and whole sausage to roux to start cooking gumbo)

Add the liquid seasonings

Add the water and remaining chicken stock (Figure 12 - Add remaining liquid to gumbo)

Bring to a boil then lower the heat to simmer for about 45 minutes

If too thick, add more water

Skim off any fat or residue that rises to the surface

After 45 minutes, remove the sausage, cut into ¼ inch thick slices, then return to the pot (Figure 13 – After cooking for 45 minutes, remove sausage and slice)

Remove the chicken thighs, cut the meat from the bone, then return the meat to the pot (Figure 14 - Remove chicken and slice)

Gumbo is ready to serve (Figure 15 - Gumbo is done)

Serve in a bowl with rice

This is a soup, so don’t add too much rice so that it becomes the consistency of a stew (Figure 16 - Serve with rice)

Shrimp Kew

By Great Chefs / Wong Brothers

Ingredients:

Gravy/Slurry

  • Salt – ½ teaspoon
  • Sugar – ½ teaspoon
  • Oyster Sauce – 1 teaspoon
  • Light Soy Sauce – 1 teaspoon
  • ½ teaspoon sesame oil
  • ½ teaspoon peanut oil
  • ½ cup chicken stock
  • Cornstarch – 1½ teaspoons, dissolved in the chicken stock

Shrimp and Vegetables

  • Shrimp – 12 oz medium or large, peeled and deveined
  • Cornstarch – 1½ teaspoons
  • Egg White – ½, beaten
  • ½ tablespoon sesame oil
  • ½ tablespoon peanut oil
  • Salt – Pinch
  • White Pepper – Pinch
  • Peanut Oil for frying the shrimp – ½ cup
  • Peanut Oil for frying the vegetables – 3 tablespoons
  • Ginger – ½ teaspoon, minced
  • Garlic – ½ teaspoon, minced
  • Celery – ½ cup cut diagonally in bite size pieces
  • Straw Mushrooms – ½ cup
  • Baby Corn – ½ cup, halved
  • Carrot – 1, sliced diagonally or fancy flower cut
  • Snow Peas – ½ cup, strings removed
  • Optional: ½ cup bell pepper, cut square in bite size pieces
  • Optional: ½ cup onion, cut square in bite size pieces
  • Rice wine – 2 tablespoons

Instructions:

  • To prepare the gravy/slurry: Mix all ingredients together and set aside
  • To prepare the shrimp and vegetables: Mix the shrimp with the cornstarch, egg white, peanut and sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat, cover, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  • Heat ½ cup peanut oil in a wok. Add the shrimp and stir-fry until they are almost completely cooked (2 to 4 minutes). Remove the shrimp and place on a plate and drain the wok.
  • Put the wok back over medium-high heat and add 3 tablespoons of peanut oil. Toss in the ginger and garlic.
  • Add all the vegetables to the wok and stir-fry for 2 to 4 minutes.
  • Add the shrimp and stir-fry 2 minutes.
  • Sprinkle in the rice wine and stir fry for another minute.
  • Add the gravy mixture and stir gently until the sauce thickens.
  • Remove from heat and serve.

Ed’s Beef Pepper Steak

By Ed Becnel

Ingredients:

1 lb flank steak, lean chuck roast, or top round sliced thinly across the grain

1 green bell pepper cut into 1 inch squares

1 red bell pepper cut into 1 inch squares

1 yellow onion cut into 1 inch squares

3 cloves of minced garlic

3/4 cup of beef broth and 3/4 cup chicken broth mixed together

1 rounded TBSP of cornstarch

1 TBSP granulated or powdered garlic

3 TBSP gluten-free soy sauce

1 TBSP oyster sauce

2 teaspoons of toasted sesame oil

Black pepper

3 TBSP peanut oil

Directions:

  • Make the slurry: Mix cornstarch with ½ cup of the broth, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and black pepper. Stir well to dissolve. Set aside.
  • Heat 1 TBSP peanut oil in a wok or skillet over high heat
  • Add 1 clove of minced garlic
  • Add half of the beef and stir fry. Add 1 TSP sesame oil and continue to stir fry until cooked through
  • Remove any liquid that appears in the wok and reserve (it will be added back later)
  • Remove the cooked beef
  • Add ½ cup of the broth to the wok, stir well, drain, and reserve the liquid (it will be added back later)
  • Re-heat the wok and follow steps 2 through 7 to cook the other half of the beef
  • Add the remaining 1 TBSP peanut oil and re-heat in the wok or pan over high heat

How to Clean and Roast Kalabasa Seeds

Kalabasa (Filipino squash) seeds can be roasted into a delicious, crunchy snack that’s full of flavor and nutrients. They can be eaten with the shell on or peeled for the green inner kernels, depending on your preference.

Step 1. Separate and Clean the Seeds

  • Scoop out the seeds from the kalabasa cavity with a spoon.
  • Place them in a large bowl of water.
  • Use your fingers to loosen and remove the stringy fibers and pulp. Seeds will float while the pulp sinks.
  • Skim out the seeds and transfer them to a colander.
  • Rinse thoroughly under running water to remove any remaining bits.
  • Pat dry with paper towels or spread on a clean towel for 15–30 minutes until dry to the touch.

Step 2. Season the Seeds

Once dry, toss the seeds in a small bowl with:

  • 1 tsp oil or melted butter per cup of seeds (for crisping)
  • ½ tsp salt, or to taste

Optional seasonings:

  • Sweet: sugar and cinnamon
  • Savory: garlic powder, paprika, or Cajun spice
  • Spicy: chili powder or cayenne pepper

Mix well until evenly coated.

Step 3. Roast the Seeds

  • Preheat your convection oven to 180°C (350°F).
  • Line a baking sheet with foil or parchment paper.
  • Spread the seeds in a single layer.
  • Roast for 12–18 minutes, stirring halfway through.
  • Watch closely after 10 minutes — the seeds brown quickly near the end.
  • The seeds are done when lightly golden and crisp.

Step 4. Cool and Store

  • Let cool completely — they’ll crisp more as they cool.
  • Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 weeks, or freeze for longer storage.

Can You Eat the Shells?

Yes! The outer shells of roasted kalabasa seeds are thin and edible. After roasting, they become crisp and flavorful. Most people eat them whole, just like pumpkin seeds, for maximum crunch, fiber, and nutrients.

If you prefer to eat only the green inner kernels (pepitas):

  • Soak the cleaned raw seeds in warm water overnight.
  • Drain and gently press each seed to loosen and remove the white shell.
  • Dry and roast the green kernels at 170°C (340°F) for 8–10 minutes until lightly golden.

This yields a nuttier, smoother flavor ideal for toppings or baking, but takes more effort.

Tips

  • Roast and eat whole for the easiest and most flavorful snack.
  • Rotate the pan halfway through roasting for even browning.
  • Add a pinch of salt before roasting to enhance natural flavor.
  • For extra crunch, let the cleaned seeds dry overnight before roasting.

How to Roast Kalabasa for Pumpkin Pie

These instructions will help you roast Filipino kalabasa squash perfectly in a convection oven with both top and bottom heating elements. The roasted flesh will be sweet, smooth, and ideal for making pie purée.

Step 1. Choose and Prepare the Kalabasa

  • Choose a mature, deep orange kalabasa with firm flesh and dull skin.
  • Wash and dry the squash thoroughly.
  • Cut off the stem and bottom ends to create flat surfaces.
  • Stand it upright and carefully cut it in half vertically from top to bottom using a heavy knife.
  • Scoop out the seeds and stringy pulp with a spoon. (Optional: save and roast the seeds.)
  • Cut each half into wedges or chunks about 2–3 inches thick for even roasting.

Step 2. Preheat the Oven

  • Set the convection oven to 200°C (about 400°F).
  • Enable both top and bottom heating elements so the air circulation cooks the squash evenly.
  • If your oven has a fan or convection mode, turn it on to help caramelize the surface.

Step 3. Prepare the Pan

  • Line a baking sheet with foil or parchment paper and lightly oil it.
  • Optionally brush the cut surfaces of the squash with a small amount of oil or melted butter for flavor.

Step 4. Roast the Kalabasa

  • Arrange the pieces cut side down (skin side up) on the baking sheet to trap steam and soften the flesh.
  • Roast for 30–45 minutes depending on the size of the pieces:
  • Smaller pieces: 25–30 minutes
  • Larger halves: 45–60 minutes
  • The kalabasa is done when the skin is slightly blistered, a fork slides easily into the flesh, and the cut sides have caramelized brown spots.

Step 5. Cool and Purée

  • Allow the roasted kalabasa to cool until safe to handle.
  • Scoop out the soft flesh from the skin with a spoon.
  • Purée the flesh in a blender or food processor until smooth.
  • If the purée is watery, simmer it gently in a saucepan for 5–10 minutes to thicken before using.

Tips

  • Roasting enhances sweetness and flavor compared to steaming.
  • Rotate the pan halfway through roasting to ensure even cooking.
  • A small pinch of salt before roasting helps bring out the natural sweetness.

Vietnamese Rice Noodle Bowl with Lemongrass Beef and Shrimp Dumplings

A fresh, vibrant noodle bowl featuring lemongrass-marinated beef, shrimp dumplings, and crisp vegetables — served with a sweet-sour Vietnamese dressing (nước chấm).

Components

  • Lemongrass Beef
  • Shrimp Dumplings
  • Rice Vermicelli
  • Fresh Garnishes (cucumber, carrots, chilis, herbs)
  • Vietnamese Dressing (nước chấm)

Ingredients (2–4 servings)

Lemongrass Beef

  • 250–300 g beef (flank, sirloin, or other tender cut), thinly sliced
  • 1 stalk lemongrass (white portion only), finely minced or pounded
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce (optional)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • A pinch of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon neutral oil (optional, for pan-searing)

Shrimp Dumplings

  • 250 g shrimp, peeled, deveined, finely chopped
  • 1 small shallot, minced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • Pinch of salt and pepper
  • Dumpling wrappers (wonton or round gyoza-style)

Rice Noodles

  • 200–250 g rice vermicelli (bún) or thin rice noodles

Garnishes

  • 1 small cucumber, thinly sliced or julienned
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Fresh chilis (thinly sliced)
  • Optional: mint or Thai basil, bean sprouts, shredded lettuce

Nước Chấm (Sweet-Sour Dressing)

  • ¼ cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (or lemon juice)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 small red chili, sliced thin
  • Optional: a few shredded carrot strips

Method

  • Marinate & Cook the Lemongrass Beef
  • Combine beef with lemongrass, fish sauce, soy sauce, garlic, sugar, and black pepper.
  • Marinate for at least 15 minutes (up to 2 hours refrigerated).
  • Heat a skillet or wok over medium-high heat with a little oil.
  • Stir-fry the beef until just cooked and slightly caramelized. Set aside.
  • Make Shrimp Dumplings
  • In a bowl, mix shrimp, shallot, garlic, fish sauce, sugar, salt, and pepper.
  • Place about 1 teaspoon filling in each wrapper. Moisten edges and fold to seal.
  • Cook by one of the following:
  • Boil: 3–4 minutes until dumplings float.
  • Steam: 6–8 minutes until opaque.
  • Pan-fry & steam (potsticker style): Brown bottoms, add a splash of water, cover to steam until cooked.Set aside once cooked.
  • Prepare Rice Noodles
  • Cook noodles per package directions (soak or boil until tender). Drain and rinse under cool water.
  • Make the Nước Chấm Dressing
  • Dissolve sugar in warm water.
  • Add lime juice and fish sauce.
  • Stir in minced garlic and sliced chili.
  • Taste and adjust to balance sweet, salty, and sour.
  • Assemble the Bowls
  • Place a portion of noodles in each bowl.
  • Arrange lemongrass beef and shrimp dumplings on top.
  • Add cucumber, carrot, chilis, and cilantro (plus any optional herbs).
  • Drizzle with several spoonfuls of nước chấm or serve on the side for dipping.
  • Toss gently before eating.
  • Optional Toppings
  • Toasted peanuts, fried garlic, or fried shallots
  • Crushed roasted rice
  • A drizzle of sesame oil or chili oil for depth

Mushroom Couscous with Mushroom Powder & Button Mushrooms

This couscous dish is a light and flavorful side that pairs beautifully with Osso Buco alla Milanese. The mushroom powder deepens the umami while sautéed button mushrooms add texture and aroma. It’s a quicker, lighter alternative to risotto or polenta.

Ingredients (Serves 4)

1 cup couscous (medium-grain or Moroccan)

1 cup vegetable or chicken stock (hot, for steaming couscous)

1 Tbsp olive oil or unsalted butter

½ cup button mushrooms, cleaned and thinly sliced

1 tsp mushroom powder (shiitake, porcini, or homemade)

1 small shallot or ½ onion, finely minced

1 clove garlic, minced

2 Tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Salt & freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Optional: 1 Tbsp butter and/or grated Parmesan for richness

Instructions

1. Prepare couscous

Bring 1 cup stock to a boil.

Stir in couscous, 1 tsp olive oil (or butter), and a pinch of salt.

Cover tightly, remove from heat, and let steam 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.

2. Cook mushrooms

In a skillet, heat 1 Tbsp olive oil over medium heat.

Add shallot (or onion) and garlic, cook until softened (2–3 minutes).

Add sliced button mushrooms, sauté until golden and slightly crisped (5–6 minutes).

Season lightly with salt and pepper.

3. Add mushroom powder

Stir mushroom powder into the sautéed mushrooms and cook 1 minute more to release aroma.

4. Combine

Fold mushroom mixture into fluffed couscous.

Add parsley and adjust seasoning.

For extra richness, stir in 1 Tbsp butter and/or a sprinkle of Parmesan.

Serving Notes

A lighter alternative to risotto or polenta, perfect with rich braised meats.

Mushroom powder deepens the umami flavor without overpowering.

Optional squeeze of lemon juice at the end adds brightness to complement gremolata.
